Make A Difference Hackathon

This 24 hour hackathon hosted by the College of Computing gives individuals a chance to produce big or small solutions for individual or global needs. We believe technology has the power to spark positive change in out communities. Our missions is to empower and enrich humanity. We want to gather undergraduates students of all backgrounds to bridge the gap between technology and social advocacy in the hopes of inspiring individuals to build creative tools that can be used to tackle society's biggest issues. Our community welcomes everyone, including all levels of programming skills. We encourage all students to join.

Details

Klaus Atrium. Registration begins at 11 AM on Saturday, January 27th. The event will conclude at 2 PM on Sunday, January 28th.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is where you turn your ideas into real stuff!
Who can apply?
Any current College of Computing undergraduate student.
What if I don't know how to code?
Experience isn't required! Hackathons are a place to learn, for anybody at any level. We'll even have alumni to assist and mentor you as you work on your project.
What if I don't have an idea or a team?
Not to worry! If you don't have a team make note of it during registration and we will match you with one!
What should I bring?
You should bring your student ID and anything you'd need for a productive and healthy weekend: laptop, charger, deodorant(!), change of clothes, etc. You won't need food or drinks -- we've got that covered for you!
What can I make?
Anything you want!
What does it cost?
The event is free, thanks so support from our sponsors!
I want to help, not hack!
If you're contemplating judging, volunteering, or mentoring, shoot us an email at hackathon@cc.gatech.edu.
